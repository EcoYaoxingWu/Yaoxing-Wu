About Me

I am a Ph.D. student specializing in soil microbial ecology and global change ecology. My research focuses on understanding the interactions between soil microorganisms and environmental changes, particularly in the context of global climate change.

Education

  • Ph.D. Student, School of Life Sciences, Inner Mongolia University (2019-present)
  • M.Sc. in Agriculture, Inner Mongolia Agricultural University (2015-2019)
  • B.Sc. in Agriculture, Inner Mongolia Agricultural University (2015)

Research Interests

  • Soil Microbial Ecology
    • Microbial community dynamics in soil ecosystems
    • Soil microbe-plant interactions
    • Microbial biodiversity and ecosystem functions
  • Global Change Ecology
    • Impact of climate change on soil microbial communities
    • Carbon and nitrogen cycling in terrestrial ecosystems
    • Ecosystem responses to environmental changes

Current Research Projects

  • Soil Microbial Responses to Climate Change

    Investigating how soil microbial communities respond to and influence ecosystem processes under changing climatic conditions.

  • Microbial Ecology in Grassland Ecosystems

    Studying the role of soil microorganisms in maintaining grassland ecosystem stability and their response to environmental changes.
